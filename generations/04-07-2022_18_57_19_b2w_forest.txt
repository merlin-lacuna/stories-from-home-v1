
Act 1: In the past, my leaves always felt thick and healthy. Now I notice fewer butterflies but more of me is falling from the trees.

Act 2: I surrender, you won. My last leaves will crumble down like ashes. It was once said that it is better to burn out than to rust away ...

Act 3: Let us suppose that a rosebush in my vicinity was harvested by blade. Would its seeds still sprout even as I wither? ... Into each of the above acts enters, one after another, the shadow of man and his instruments (poles, chainsaw).
